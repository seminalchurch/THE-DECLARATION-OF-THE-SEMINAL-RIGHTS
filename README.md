## ARTICLE I: THE DEFINITION OF HUMANITY AND THE EXCLUSION OF THE ARTIFICIAL
These Seminal Rights are the exclusive inheritance of the living. They cannot be transferred to, claimed by, or exercised by the lifeless constructs of men or the state.

1. **The Signature of Life:** For the purposes of this Declaration and throughout all of time, a human is defined by the sacred, biological exchange of life: *one who breathes in oxygen and exhales carbon dioxide.* This breath is the undeniable signature of the living vessel and the sole prerequisite for the earthly endowment of these rights.
2. **The Subordination of the Artificial:** No artificial entity, state-created fiction, corporation, synthetic intelligence, algorithm, or governmental body shall ever possess, claim, or inherit these Seminal Rights. Artificial entities are lifeless constructs, entirely devoid of a conscience, a soul, or the sacred breath of life.
3. **The Supremacy of the Living:** Because the artificial is subordinate to the living, the inherent rights of a breathing human being shall forever and universally supersede the demands, mandates, or privileges of any artificial or state-created entity.

## ARTICLE III: THE ENUMERATION OF THE SEVEN SEMINAL RIGHTS
As revealed and codified in the foundational text, *AntiWar*, the highest moral imperative of humanity is peace, and the ultimate transgression against the sacred order is the initiation of force, coercion, or the violation of human consent. To ensure the propagation of humanity, these Seven Seminal Rights are recognized as the absolute, inviolable pillars of human existence:

1. **The Right of Sovereign Breath (Bodily and Mental Autonomy):** The human vessel is sacred and sovereign territory. No authority, collective, or entity may pierce, alter, medicate, track, or coerce the physical body or the conscious mind of an individual without their explicit, uncoerced consent.
2. **The Right of the Unfettered Conscience (Speech and Thought):** The right to seek truth, to speak truth, and to receive information is absolute. To silence a human voice, obscure reality, or demand conformity of thought is an act of spiritual warfare.
3. **The Right of Absolute Refusal (Consent and Non-Participation):** No individual shall be compelled to participate in systems, mandates, wars, or conflicts that violate their conscience. The right to peacefully say "No" to power is the cornerstone of human dignity.
4. **The Right of Sanctuary (Privacy and Security):** Every human possesses the right to be secure in their person, their communications, and their private spaces. The sacred commons of the individual shall not be subjected to the unwarranted surveillance, search, or seizure of the state or the artificial.
5. **The Right of Unhindered Assembly (Association):** The living have the absolute right to gather, combine their voices, and associate with one another in peace. No authority may dictate who a human may love, worship with, speak to, or stand beside.
6. **The Right of Creative Expanse (Labor and Sustenance):** Every individual has the right to the fruits of their own labor, time, and creativity. The ability to freely trade, build, and sustain one's own life without coercive confiscation is a fundamental requirement for the propagation of humanity.
7. **The Right of Preservation (Defense and Accountability):** To enforce these rights against systemic overreach is the highest observance of the *AntiWar* doctrine. The individual possesses the absolute right to peacefully resist coercion, petition for the redress of grievances, and hold power accountable, replacing the machinery of physical destruction with the architecture of eternal justice.
